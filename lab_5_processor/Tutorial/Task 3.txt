Background:
We have finally reached it, the big kahuna. In this section we are going to make the brains of the
operation, the controller. Everything ends up becoming a state in our machine and adding custom
instructions is as simple as adding more states to the machine. This makes the controller mostly
mindless grunt work, since the FSM states themselves are relatively simple. Our FSM models a multi-
cycle CPU with an average CPI of roughly 5 clock cycles.

It will need more “wait” states in some areas due to latency with memories and the ALU (see notes below). You only need to create those instructions referenced in the assembly program. The main loop:
- It starts at fetch, which points to decode.
- decode splits into three main branches: Rops, Iops, and Jops.
- Everything eventually funnels down into finish, which loops all the way back up to fetch.
Branch 1: Rops (R-Type Operations)
From Rops, the system can transition into six different states:
- calc (goes to store)
- jr (goes to store)
- recv* (has a self-loop, and goes to store)
- rpix* (goes to store)
- wpix* (goes directly to finish)
- send* (has a self-loop, and goes directly to finish)
Branch 2: Iops (I-Type Operations)
From Iops, the system can transition into five different states:
- equals (goes to store)
- nequal (goes to store)
- ori (goes to store)
- lw (goes to store)
- sw (goes directly to finish)
Branch 3: Jops (J-Type Operations)
From Jops, the system can transition into three different states:
- jmp (goes directly to finish)
- jal (goes directly to finish)
- clrscr* (goes directly to finish)
The Funnel States:
- store: All the states that calculate or read data (calc, jr, recv*, rpix*, equals, nequal, ori, lw) point to store. From store, it goes to finish.
- finish: All the write/jump states (wpix*, send*, sw, jmp, jal, clrscr*) point directly here, along with store.
NOTE 1: Reading from irMem and dMem has a 1 clock cycle latency. Your results will not be available
until the next rising edge after you assert your control signals. This 1 cycle latency also exists for reading
the result from your ALU
NOTE 2: Any control signals asserted should be immediately de-asserted on the next state in your state
machine
